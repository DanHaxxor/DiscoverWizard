Honest Assessment
If nothing changes

Revenue stays flat and the team keeps burning time on busywork instead of selling.

Any CRM will help here — the key is adoption, not the tool. Zoho CRM works well for this, but if your team already knows HubSpot or Salesforce, switching costs may outweigh the savings. Start a 15-day trial and see if it clicks before committing.

Hesitation about switching

The team tried a CRM two years ago and nobody used it after the first month.

This is a people problem, not a software problem. No tool fixes low adoption on its own. Zoho's UI is simpler than Salesforce, but more complex than HubSpot's free tier. If ease-of-use is the #1 concern, HubSpot may be a better fit. If budget and long-term flexibility matter more, Zoho gives you more for less.

After committing

Worried about outgrowing the setup or getting stuck if support is slow.

Zoho scales well up to mid-market. Enterprise-grade needs (50+ sales reps, complex territory rules, dedicated CSM) are where Salesforce pulls ahead. Support is responsive on paid plans but slower on free tiers. Be honest about where you'll be in 2 years — if the answer is 100+ users with complex workflows, plan your exit path now.

Potential incompatibilities

You mentioned using spreadsheets and manual emails today.

Zoho Campaigns has stricter sending limits than Mailchimp or SendGrid if you're doing high-volume cold outreach. If your email list is unverified or cold, deliverability tools like Instantly or Apollo may serve you better for that specific use case. Zoho is strongest when your contacts are warm and already in your pipeline.
