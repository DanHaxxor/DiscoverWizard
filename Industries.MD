# Industry Taxonomy

---

## Advocacy & Membership Groups

Organizations in the Advocacy & Membership Groups industry are primarily involved in promoting specific causes or representing member interests through organized activities. Examples include trade associations, labor unions, professional societies, and social advocacy groups.

### Business, Professional, Labor, & Political Organizations

Organizations primarily involved in advancing the economic or professional interests of their members. This includes trade associations, chambers of commerce, labor unions, professional societies, and political parties that represent and advocate for their constituents' business or career interests.

### Civic & Social Organizations

Organizations primarily involved in promoting the social and recreational interests of their members through fellowship and activities. This includes alumni associations, fraternal lodges, social clubs, parent-teacher associations, and scouting organizations that bring members together for shared interests.

### Social Advocacy Organizations

Organizations primarily involved in promoting specific social or political causes to benefit society or targeted groups. This includes environmental advocacy groups, human rights organizations, conservation groups, and other nonprofits that campaign for social change through education, lobbying, and public awareness.

## Agriculture

Organizations in the Agriculture industry are primarily involved in growing crops, raising animals, harvesting timber, and catching fish or wildlife for commercial purposes. This includes farms, ranches, orchards, and fishing operations.

### Animal Farming & Wildlife Harvesting

Organizations primarily involved in raising livestock for meat, dairy, or other animal products, as well as catching fish and other wild animals. This encompasses cattle ranches, poultry farms, dairy operations, commercial fishing, and aquaculture facilities.

### Crop Production

Organizations primarily involved in growing plants for food, fiber, or other uses on farms, orchards, greenhouses, and nurseries. This includes grain farming, fruit and vegetable growing, greenhouse operations, and the cultivation of any plant-based agricultural products.

### Forestry & Logging

Organizations primarily involved in growing and harvesting timber on long production cycles of 10 years or more. This includes timber harvesting operations, forest nurseries producing tree seedlings, and services supporting forest management like fire prevention and reforestation.

---

## Business Consulting & Development

Organizations in the Business Consulting & Development industry are primarily involved in providing strategic advisory services to help organizations improve their overall performance and achieve business objectives. Unlike firms offering specific functional services (legal, accounting, etc.), these consultants analyze entire business operations and provide guidance on management strategy, organizational structure, market positioning, and operational efficiency.

### Business Administration & Management Consultants

Organizations primarily involved in providing broad operational advice to help businesses run more efficiently. This includes general management consultants, strategic planning advisors, financial management consultants, and firms that help with organizational structure, site selection, or business process improvement.

### Human Resources Consultants

Organizations primarily involved in advising businesses on employee-related policies and practices. This includes compensation and benefits consultants, employee assessment services, HR policy advisors, and firms that help design personnel management systems and wage structures.

### Marketing Consultants

Organizations primarily involved in helping businesses develop and implement marketing strategies to reach customers effectively. This includes market research consultants, brand strategy advisors, new product development consultants, pricing specialists, and firms that guide sales and marketing planning.

### Misc. Consulting Services

Organizations primarily involved in providing specialized management advice not covered by other categories. This includes telecommunications consultants, utilities management advisors, and other niche consulting services that help businesses with specific technical or operational challenges.

### Process & Logistics Consultants

Organizations primarily involved in optimizing how businesses produce and deliver their products. This includes manufacturing efficiency experts, supply chain consultants, inventory management advisors, transportation logistics specialists, and firms focused on improving operational workflows.

---

## Construction

Organizations in the Construction industry are primarily involved in building new structures and infrastructure or renovating existing ones. This covers residential and commercial buildings, roads, bridges, and utilities.

### Commercial/Government Building Construction

Organizations primarily involved in constructing non-residential buildings like offices, stores, schools, hospitals, and government facilities. This includes general contractors specializing in commercial projects, design-build firms, and companies that oversee the construction of institutional and public buildings.

### Heavy & Civil Engineering Construction

Organizations primarily involved in building large-scale infrastructure projects and engineering works. This includes companies constructing highways, bridges, dams, utility lines, pipelines, and other major civil engineering projects that form the backbone of public infrastructure.

### Residential Building Construction

Organizations primarily involved in building or renovating houses, apartments, and other residential structures. This includes single-family home builders, multifamily developers, residential remodeling contractors, and companies that manage the overall construction of homes from start to finish.

### Specialty Trade Contractors

Organizations primarily involved in performing specific construction tasks rather than managing entire projects. This includes plumbers, electricians, carpenters, concrete contractors, roofers, and other specialized trades that typically work as subcontractors on larger construction projects.

---

## Eating & Drinking Establishments

Organizations in the Eating & Drinking Establishments industry are primarily involved in preparing and serving food and beverages for immediate consumption. This covers restaurants, bars, cafeterias, food trucks, and catering services.

### Bars & Pubs

Organizations primarily involved in preparing and serving alcoholic beverages for immediate consumption on the premises. This includes taverns, nightclubs, cocktail lounges, and drinking establishments that may also offer limited food service as a secondary activity.

### Caterers & Food Trucks

Organizations primarily involved in preparing and serving food at locations chosen by the customer or from mobile vehicles. This includes event catering services, food trucks, mobile ice cream vendors, and hot dog carts that bring prepared food directly to customers.

### Restaurants & Cafeterias

Organizations primarily involved in providing meals and snacks for immediate consumption in sit-down or quick-service settings. This includes full-service restaurants with table service, fast-food establishments, cafeterias, coffee shops, and specialty snack bars like ice cream parlors.

---

## Education & Training

Organizations in the Education & Training industry are primarily involved in teaching and instructing students through schools, colleges, training centers, and other educational programs. This industry covers everything from kindergarten through university and professional development.

### Arts, Recreation, & Skills Instruction

Organizations primarily involved in teaching creative, athletic, or personal enrichment skills outside formal academic programs. This includes music schools, art studios, dance academies, sports instruction, driving schools, and language learning centers.

### Business Courses & Professional Training

Organizations primarily involved in providing short-term training to enhance business and administrative skills. This includes computer training centers, management development programs, secretarial schools, and providers of professional certification courses.

### Colleges, Universities, & Professional Schools

Organizations primarily involved in providing education at the bachelor's degree level and above. This includes four-year colleges, research universities, graduate schools, and professional schools for law, medicine, business, and other advanced fields.

### Elementary & Secondary Schools

Organizations primarily involved in providing basic education from kindergarten through 12th grade. This includes public school districts, private schools, charter schools, parochial schools, military academies, and specialized schools for students with disabilities.

### Junior Colleges

Organizations primarily involved in providing two-year post-secondary education leading to associate degrees or certificates below the bachelor's level. This includes community colleges, technical colleges, and junior colleges that prepare students for careers or transfer to four-year institutions.

### Technical & Trade Schools

Organizations primarily involved in teaching specific occupational skills that lead to job-ready certifications. This includes cosmetology schools, automotive training centers, culinary institutes, nursing programs, truck driving schools, and apprenticeship programs.

### Testing, Guidance Counseling, & Other Educational Support Services

Organizations primarily involved in supporting education without directly teaching. This includes standardized testing services, educational consultants, guidance counseling services, student exchange programs, and organizations that help schools improve their programs.

---

## Engineering & Technical Services

Organizations in the Engineering & Technical Services industry are primarily involved in applying scientific and technical knowledge to design solutions and solve specialized problems. Key services include architectural design, engineering consulting, laboratory testing, and environmental assessment.

### Architectural & Engineering Services

Organizations primarily involved in planning and designing buildings and infrastructure projects, as well as providing related technical services. This includes architectural firms, civil engineering companies, structural design consultants, surveying and mapping services, and building inspection services.

### Interior, Industrial, & Graphic Design Services

Organizations primarily involved in creating aesthetic and functional designs for spaces, products, and visual communications. This includes interior design firms, industrial product designers, graphic design studios, and fashion designers who focus on the visual and practical aspects of their specialized fields.

### Misc. Technical Services

Organizations primarily involved in providing specialized technical services not covered in other categories. This includes commercial photography studios, veterinary services, translation and interpretation services, and other professional services requiring specific technical expertise.

### R&D Services

Organizations primarily involved in conducting original research and experimental development to advance scientific knowledge or create new products and processes. This includes biotechnology research labs, materials science facilities, pharmaceutical research organizations, and other establishments where scientists work to make new discoveries.

### Technical Consulting Services

Organizations primarily involved in providing expert advice on scientific and technical matters to help businesses solve problems. This includes environmental consultants, safety consultants, agricultural advisors, energy efficiency experts, and other specialists who apply technical knowledge to practical business challenges.

## Entertainment & Recreation

Organizations in the Entertainment & Recreation industry are primarily involved in providing fun, leisure, and cultural experiences through live performances, sports events, amusement parks, museums, and recreational facilities. This industry comprises the organized provision of cultural, athletic, and recreational activities intended to inform, engage, and entertain the public.

### Amusement Parks & Arcades

Organizations primarily involved in operating facilities with rides, games, and attractions for entertainment. This includes theme parks, water parks, amusement piers, arcade game centers, and similar venues designed for fun and recreation.

### Cultural, Historical, & Nature Sites

Organizations primarily involved in preserving and displaying objects or places of cultural, historical, or natural significance. This includes museums, art galleries, historical sites, botanical gardens, zoos, and nature parks that educate and inspire visitors.

### Event Promoters

Organizations primarily involved in organizing, marketing, and managing live events for public attendance. This includes concert promoters, sports event organizers, festival coordinators, and companies that handle the business side of bringing entertainment to audiences.

### Gambling Industries

Organizations primarily involved in operating gambling facilities or providing gambling services where people wager money on games of chance. This includes casinos, card rooms, bingo halls, lottery operators, and off-track betting establishments.

### Independent Artists

Organizations primarily involved in creating or performing artistic work as self-employed individuals rather than through companies. This includes freelance actors, musicians, writers, visual artists, and athletes who work independently or make public appearances for fees.

### Leisure/Recreational Facilities & Services

Organizations primarily involved in operating venues or providing services for sports, fitness, and recreational activities. This includes golf courses, fitness centers, bowling alleys, marinas, ski resorts, and country clubs where people pursue leisure activities.

### Performing Arts Companies

Organizations primarily involved in producing and presenting live artistic performances for audiences. This includes theater companies, dance troupes, symphony orchestras, opera companies, comedy clubs, and other groups that create and stage live entertainment.

### Spectator Sports

Organizations primarily involved in presenting athletic competitions for paying audiences or operating the venues where these events occur. This includes professional sports teams, racing organizations, independent athletes competing publicly, and racetracks that host competitive events.

### Talent Agents & Managers

Organizations primarily involved in representing and guiding the careers of performers, athletes, and other public figures. This includes talent agencies, artist management firms, sports agents, and booking agents who negotiate contracts and opportunities for their clients.

---

## Facility & Operational Support Services

Organizations in the Facility & Operational Support Services industry are primarily involved in maintaining physical facilities and supporting daily business operations. This industry includes security services, janitorial companies, landscaping firms, and comprehensive facility management.

### Building & Residential Maintenance Services

Organizations primarily involved in cleaning and maintaining buildings and outdoor spaces. This includes janitorial services, carpet cleaning, landscaping, pest control, and other services that keep residential and commercial properties in good condition.

### Full-Service Facility Management

Organizations primarily involved in providing comprehensive operational support by supplying staff to handle multiple facility services at client locations. This includes companies that provide integrated services like janitorial, security, mail handling, maintenance, and reception through on-site teams that manage day-to-day facility operations.

### Investigation, Security, & Alarm Services

Organizations primarily involved in protecting people and property through security personnel and systems. This includes guard services, private investigators, armored car services, alarm system installation and monitoring, and other specialized security operations.

### Misc. Facility & Operational Support Services

Organizations primarily involved in providing specialized operational support that doesn't fit other categories. This includes unique services like convention planning assistance, parking management, and other day-to-day business support functions not covered by standard facility services.

---

## Finance

Organizations in the Finance industry are primarily involved in managing money through banking, lending, investing, and trading financial products. This industry includes banks, credit unions, investment firms, and financial markets.

### Banking & Lending

Organizations primarily involved in accepting deposits and making loans, or facilitating credit through other means. This includes commercial banks, credit unions, savings institutions, mortgage companies, credit card issuers, and loan brokers that connect borrowers with lenders.

### Funds, Trusts, & Employee Benefits

Organizations primarily involved in pooling money from multiple investors to invest according to specific strategies. This includes mutual funds, pension funds, hedge funds, and other investment vehicles that have little to no employees but manage large pools of assets for shareholders or beneficiaries.

### Monetary Authorities--Central Bank

Organizations primarily involved in performing central banking functions like issuing currency, managing national money supply, holding reserves for other banks, and acting as the government's fiscal agent. This includes federal reserve banks and other institutions that control monetary policy at the national level.

### Securities, Commodities, & Investing

Organizations primarily involved in buying and selling financial products, underwriting securities, or managing investments. This includes stock brokerages, investment banks, commodity dealers, asset management firms, and financial advisors who help clients invest their money.

---

## Government

Organizations in the Government industry are primarily involved in creating laws, providing public services, and managing public programs at federal, state, and local levels. This industry consists of the governmental entities responsible for lawmaking, administration, and the operation of programs and services that maintain public order and manage societal functions.

### Administration of Economic Programs

Agencies primarily involved in managing government programs that promote economic development and regulate commerce. This includes agencies overseeing trade, small business development, labor relations, transportation policy, and economic statistics.

### Administration of Environmental Quality Programs

Agencies primarily involved in protecting and managing the environment through government regulation and programs. This includes environmental protection agencies, conservation departments, and offices that monitor pollution, enforce environmental laws, and manage natural resources.

### Administration of Housing Programs, Urban Planning, & Community Development

Agencies primarily involved in managing government programs for housing and community improvement. This includes public housing authorities, urban planning departments, community development agencies, and offices that oversee building codes and zoning.

### Administration of Human Resource Programs

Agencies primarily involved in managing government programs that provide benefits and services to workers and retirees. This includes agencies administering unemployment insurance, social security, workers' compensation, and other employment-related government benefits.

### Executive, Legislative, & Other General Government Support

Organizations primarily involved in overall governance and administration of government operations. This includes offices of mayors, governors, and presidents, legislative bodies like congress and city councils, and agencies that manage government finances and general administration.

### Justice, Public Order, & Safety Activities

Organizations primarily involved in maintaining law and order and administering justice. This includes police departments, fire departments, courts, correctional facilities, and other agencies responsible for public safety and the legal system.

### National Security & International Affairs

Organizations primarily involved in protecting national interests and managing relationships with other countries. This includes military forces, defense departments, intelligence agencies, diplomatic services, and agencies managing foreign aid and international relations.

### Space Research & Technology

Organizations primarily involved in government space exploration and research activities. This includes space agencies that conduct space missions, develop spacecraft, manage satellites, and perform space-related scientific research.

## Healthcare

Organizations in the Healthcare industry are primarily involved in providing medical care through hospitals, doctor's offices, clinics, labs, and home health services. This industry comprises the full spectrum of activities dedicated to maintaining health, preventing illness, and providing diagnostic, therapeutic, and rehabilitative services.

### Home Health Care Services

Organizations primarily involved in providing medical and personal care services in patients' homes. This includes visiting nurse services, home hospice care, home infusion therapy, and agencies that send skilled healthcare workers to treat patients at home.

### Hospitals

Organizations primarily involved in providing inpatient medical care with 24-hour staffing, specialized equipment, and comprehensive services. This includes general hospitals, specialty hospitals focusing on specific conditions, teaching hospitals, and medical centers that combine inpatient and outpatient services.

### Medical & Diagnostic Laboratories

Organizations primarily involved in analyzing body fluids and tissues or providing medical imaging services. This includes blood testing labs, pathology laboratories, X-ray centers, MRI facilities, and other diagnostic services that support medical treatment.

### Misc. Outpatient Services

Organizations primarily involved in providing ambulatory health services not covered by other categories. This includes blood banks, organ procurement organizations, mobile health screening units, and other specialized outpatient health services.

### Non-Physician Outpatient Clinics

Organizations primarily involved in providing healthcare through licensed practitioners other than medical doctors or dentists. This includes chiropractors, optometrists, physical therapists, mental health counselors, and alternative medicine practitioners operating their own clinics.

### Nursing & Residential Care Facilities

Organizations primarily involved in providing residential care combined with healthcare services. This includes nursing homes, assisted living facilities, group homes for people with disabilities, residential mental health facilities, and continuing care retirement communities.

### Outpatient Care Centers

Organizations primarily involved in providing medical services through facilities with multiple practitioners and specialized capabilities. This includes surgery centers, dialysis clinics, mental health centers, substance abuse treatment facilities, and community health centers offering comprehensive outpatient services.

### Outpatient Dentist Offices

Organizations primarily involved in providing dental care through licensed dentists operating their own practices. This includes general dentists, orthodontists, oral surgeons, and other dental specialists who diagnose and treat problems with teeth and gums.

### Outpatient Physician Offices

Organizations primarily involved in providing medical care through licensed physicians (MDs or DOs) operating private practices or clinics. This includes family doctors, specialists like cardiologists or dermatologists, multi-physician group practices, and urgent care clinics staffed by doctors.

---

## Holding Companies & Corporate Management

Organizations in the Holding Companies & Corporate Management industry are primarily involved in owning controlling stakes in other businesses or providing high-level management and strategic direction to groups of companies. These are the parent companies that oversee sophisticated corporate portfolios.

---

## Hospitality & Lodging

Organizations in the Hospitality & Lodging industry are primarily involved in providing temporary places to stay for travelers and vacationers. This includes hotels, motels, campgrounds, and other short-term accommodations.

### Boarding Houses, Dorms, & Workers' Accommodations

Organizations primarily involved in providing longer-term or transitional lodging that often serves as a principal residence during occupancy. This includes rooming houses, off-campus dormitories, workers' camps, and similar facilities that may also provide meals and housekeeping services.

### Hotels & Motels (incl Casino Hotels)

Organizations primarily involved in providing short-term lodging in facilities with guest rooms and often additional services like restaurants, meeting spaces, or gaming. This includes full-service hotels, limited-service motels, casino hotels, boutique inns, and bed-and-breakfast establishments.

### RV Parks & Recreational Camps

Organizations primarily involved in providing outdoor accommodations and recreational facilities for travelers and vacationers. This includes RV parks with hookups for recreational vehicles, campgrounds offering tent and RV sites, and recreational camps focused on outdoor activities.

---

## Insurance

Organizations in the Insurance industry are primarily involved in protecting people and businesses from financial losses by pooling risk and paying claims when covered events occur. This industry includes companies that underwrite policies and those that sell them.

### Insurance Agencies & Brokerages

Organizations primarily involved in selling insurance policies on behalf of insurance carriers without assuming the underlying risk. This includes independent insurance agents, brokers who represent multiple carriers, and agencies that help customers find and purchase appropriate coverage.

### Insurance Carriers

Organizations primarily involved in underwriting insurance policies and annuities, assuming financial risk in exchange for premium payments. This includes life insurers, health insurers, property and casualty insurers, and reinsurance companies that take on risk from other insurers.

### Supplementary Insurance Services

Organizations primarily involved in providing administrative and support services to the insurance industry without selling policies or assuming risk. This includes claims adjusters, third-party administrators who manage insurance plans, actuarial services, and other specialized insurance support functions.

---

## Manufacturing

Organizations in the Manufacturing industry are primarily involved in transforming raw materials into finished products through mechanical, chemical, or physical processes. This industry encompasses everything from food processing to electronics assembly.

### Aerospace Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in making aircraft, spacecraft, and related propulsion systems. This includes airplane manufacturers, rocket builders, helicopter makers, and producers of aerospace engines and parts.

### Apparel & Textile Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in making fabric from fibers and creating clothing and other textile products. This includes textile mills, clothing manufacturers, carpet makers, and producers of household textiles like sheets and towels.

### Beverage Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in producing drinks for human consumption. This includes breweries, wineries, soft drink manufacturers, bottled water companies, and producers of juices, coffee, and tea products.

### Cement & Concrete Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in making cement or mixing concrete for construction use. This includes cement plants, ready-mix concrete suppliers, and manufacturers of concrete blocks, pipes, and precast products.

### Chemical Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in transforming raw materials through chemical processes to create new substances. This includes producers of basic chemicals, fertilizers, plastics, synthetic materials, paints, cleaning products, and cosmetics.

### Clay & Ceramic Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in shaping and firing clay to create ceramic products. This includes brick manufacturers, pottery makers, ceramic tile producers, and makers of plumbing fixtures and refractory products.

### Computer & Electronics Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in producing electronic devices and components using specialized miniaturization technologies. This includes computer manufacturers, semiconductor producers, and makers of electronic instruments and communications equipment.

### Electrical & Lighting Equipment Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in making equipment for generating, distributing, or using electrical power. This includes manufacturers of light bulbs, lighting fixtures, electric motors, transformers, and switchgear.

### Food Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in transforming agricultural products and livestock into food for consumption. This includes meat processing plants, bakeries, dairy processors, canning facilities, and companies that prepare packaged foods for distribution.

### Furniture & Furnishings Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in making furniture and related products for homes and offices. This includes manufacturers of household furniture, office furniture, mattresses, blinds, and other furnishings.

### Glass Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in making glass and glass products by melting silica materials. This includes flat glass manufacturers, bottle makers, fiberglass producers, and specialty glass fabricators.

### Household Appliance Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in making electric appliances for home use. This includes manufacturers of refrigerators, washing machines, small kitchen appliances, and other devices that make household tasks easier.

### Leather Product Manufacturing (Real & Synthetic)

Organizations primarily involved in tanning hides and creating products from leather or leather substitutes. This includes leather tanneries and manufacturers of luggage, handbags, belts, and other leather goods except footwear.

### Machinery Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in assembling mechanical devices that apply force to do work. This includes makers of industrial machinery, farm equipment, construction machinery, and various types of mechanical equipment for commercial use.

### Medical Equipment & Supplies Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in making devices and supplies used in healthcare. This includes manufacturers of surgical instruments, dental equipment, medical implants, hospital beds, and diagnostic equipment.

### Metal Production & Fabrication

Organizations primarily involved in smelting metals and shaping them into basic forms or fabricated products. This includes steel mills, aluminum smelters, metal stamping operations, and manufacturers of structural metal products.

### Misc. Nonmetallic Mineral Product Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in processing minerals into products not covered elsewhere. This includes manufacturers of gypsum products, abrasives, mineral wool insulation, and other processed mineral products.

### Misc. Specialty Product Manufacturing (toys, office supplies, etc.)

Organizations primarily involved in making various consumer and commercial products not classified elsewhere. This includes toy manufacturers, jewelry makers, sporting goods producers, sign makers, and manufacturers of office supplies beyond paper products.

### Motor Vehicle Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in assembling cars, trucks, and vehicle bodies or trailers. This includes automobile assembly plants, truck manufacturers, RV builders, and companies making specialized vehicle bodies.

### Paper Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in making pulp, paper, and paperboard, or converting these materials into finished products. This includes paper mills, envelope manufacturers, cardboard box makers, and producers of sanitary paper products.

### Petroleum & Coal Product Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in refining crude oil or processing coal into usable products. This includes oil refineries, asphalt plants, and manufacturers of lubricants and other petroleum-based products.

### Pharmaceutical & Medicine Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in making medications and biological products for medical use. This includes drug manufacturers, vaccine producers, makers of medical botanicals, and companies producing vitamins and supplements.

### Plastic & Rubber Product Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in shaping plastics and rubber into finished products. This includes manufacturers of plastic containers, rubber tires, plastic pipes, and various molded plastic and rubber items.

### Printing (Books, Clothing, Etc.)

Organizations primarily involved in transferring text and images onto various materials through printing processes. This includes commercial printers, book publishers who print, newspaper printers, and businesses that print on clothing, packaging, and other materials.

### Tobacco Product Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in processing tobacco and making cigarettes, cigars, and other tobacco products. This includes companies that prepare tobacco leaves and manufacture smoking and chewing tobacco products.

### Train, Ship, & Misc. Transportation Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in building transportation equipment other than motor vehicles or aircraft. This includes shipbuilders, railroad equipment manufacturers, motorcycle makers, and bicycle manufacturers.

### Wood & Lumber Product Manufacturing

Organizations primarily involved in sawing logs and shaping wood into finished products. This includes sawmills, plywood manufacturers, cabinet makers, and producers of prefabricated wooden buildings and wood components.

## Media

Organizations in the Media industry are primarily involved in producing, distributing, broadcasting, or preserving content across various formats including video, audio, print, and digital. This encompasses movie and music production, publishing operations, broadcast networks, streaming platforms, and content archives.

### Digital Media & Content Providers

Organizations primarily involved in distributing content through digital channels and operating online platforms for media consumption. This includes streaming services, social media platforms, online news sites, podcasting networks, and other internet-based content delivery systems.

### Libraries & Archives

Organizations primarily involved in collecting, preserving, and providing access to books, documents, recordings, and other information resources. This includes public libraries, university libraries, corporate information centers, historical archives, and specialized collections that maintain and share knowledge resources.

### Motion Picture & Video Industry

Organizations primarily involved in producing, distributing, and exhibiting movies, TV shows, and other video content. This includes film studios, movie theaters, production companies, post-production facilities, and video distribution services that create and deliver visual entertainment.

### Music & Audio Recording Industry

Organizations primarily involved in producing and distributing musical recordings and providing related services. This includes record labels, recording studios, music publishers, and companies that produce, promote, and distribute music across various formats.

### Print Media Publishing Industry

Organizations primarily involved in publishing written content that others have created or commissioning original works for publication. This includes newspaper publishers, magazine companies, book publishers, directory publishers, and greeting card companies that create and distribute printed materials.

### Radio & TV Stations

Organizations primarily involved in broadcasting audio or video programming to the public through owned and operated transmission facilities. This includes AM/FM radio stations, television stations, and networks that create or acquire content and transmit it over the airwaves.

---

## Personal Services

Organizations in the Personal Services industry are primarily involved in addressing individual personal care and lifestyle needs through direct service. Major categories include beauty salons, funeral homes, dry cleaners, and various personal care establishments.

### Beauty, Grooming, & Wellness Services

Organizations primarily involved in providing appearance and personal care services directly to individuals. This includes hair salons, barber shops, nail salons, spas, tattoo parlors, and other establishments focused on helping people look and feel their best.

### Death Care Services

Organizations primarily involved in preparing the deceased for burial or cremation and providing memorial services. This includes funeral homes that arrange and conduct funerals, cemeteries that maintain burial grounds, crematoriums, and establishments that prepare bodies for final disposition.

### Dry-cleaning & Laundry Services

Organizations primarily involved in cleaning clothing and textiles for individuals and businesses. This includes dry cleaners, coin-operated laundromats, wash-and-fold services, and commercial laundry services that clean uniforms, linens, and other textiles on a contract basis.

### Misc. Personal Services

Organizations primarily involved in providing various personal services that don't fit other categories. This includes pet grooming, parking services, dating services, personal shopping services, and other establishments that cater to individual personal needs.

### Private Households

Organizations primarily involved in employing workers to maintain private residences and serve families. This includes households that employ maids, cooks, nannies, gardeners, butlers, and other domestic workers who provide services within private homes.

---

## Professional & Administrative Services

Organizations in the Professional & Administrative Services industry are primarily involved in providing specialized expertise and operational support on a contract basis. Common services include legal representation, accounting, advertising, personnel management, and administrative assistance.

### Accounting & Payroll Services

Organizations primarily involved in maintaining financial records, preparing tax returns, and processing employee compensation. This includes CPA firms, bookkeeping services, tax preparation companies, payroll processors, and businesses that help others manage their financial record-keeping.

### Advertising & PR

Organizations primarily involved in creating and executing promotional campaigns and managing public perception for clients. This includes advertising agencies, public relations firms, media buying services, outdoor billboard companies, and businesses that help others market their products and manage their reputation.

### Business Support Services

Organizations primarily involved in providing ongoing operational support for routine business needs. This includes call centers, document copying services, credit bureaus, collection agencies, and other services that support day-to-day business operations.

### Legal Services

Organizations primarily involved in providing legal representation, advice, and document preparation for individuals and businesses. This includes law firms, individual attorney practices, notary services, title companies, paralegal services, and other establishments offering legal expertise.

### Misc. Professional Services

Organizations primarily involved in gathering and analyzing information for business decision-making. This includes market research firms, opinion polling companies, media rating services, and businesses that collect and interpret data about markets and public opinion.

### Office Operations & Administration

Organizations primarily involved in handling routine business tasks and administrative functions on a contract basis. This includes companies providing billing services, document management, appointment scheduling, and other back-office support that keeps businesses running smoothly.

### Staffing & HR Services

Organizations primarily involved in finding workers for employers and managing human resource functions. This includes temporary staffing agencies, executive search firms, employment placement services, and companies that handle HR administration for other businesses.

### Translation & Interpretation Services

Organizations primarily involved in converting spoken or written communication from one language to another. This includes document translation services, simultaneous interpreters for meetings, sign language interpreters, and companies that help bridge language barriers in business and personal settings.

### Travel Arrangement & Reservation Services

Organizations primarily involved in planning trips and making travel bookings for individuals and businesses. This includes travel agencies, tour operators, ticket reservation services, and companies that coordinate business travel or vacation packages.

---

## Real Estate

Organizations in the Real Estate industry are primarily involved in buying, selling, renting, and managing land and buildings. This industry includes real estate agents, property managers, and companies that own and lease properties.

### Property Management, Appraisal, & Misc. Real Estate Services

Organizations primarily involved in managing properties for others or providing specialized real estate services. This includes property management companies that handle day-to-day operations for owners, real estate appraisers who determine property values, and other support services like real estate consulting.

### Real Estate Agencies & Brokerages

Organizations primarily involved in facilitating property transactions by representing buyers, sellers, or renters on a commission basis. This includes residential real estate agents, commercial brokers, and firms that help clients buy, sell, or lease properties without owning the real estate themselves.

### Real Estate Leasing

Organizations primarily involved in owning or managing properties that they rent out to tenants on a recurring basis. This includes apartment buildings, office complexes, retail spaces, warehouses, and self-storage facilities, as well as real estate investment trusts (REITs) that lease properties.

---

## Religious, Giving, & Grant Organizations

Organizations in the Religious, Giving, & Grant Organizations industry are primarily involved in operating religious institutions or managing charitable funds for social causes. This includes churches, temples, charitable foundations, and grant making organizations.

### Grant making & Giving Services

Organizations primarily involved in collecting charitable donations and distributing funds through grants to support social, educational, health, and cultural causes. This includes private foundations, community foundations, charitable trusts, and fundraising organizations that channel resources to nonprofit activities.

### Religious Organizations

Organizations primarily involved in operating places of worship and administering religious activities. This includes churches, temples, mosques, synagogues, monasteries, and religious administrative offices that organize and support faith-based practices.

---

## Rental & Leasing Services

Organizations in the Rental & Leasing Services industry are primarily involved in letting customers temporarily use equipment, vehicles, or other assets for a fee without transferring ownership. This industry includes both physical items like cars and intangible assets like franchise rights.

### Automotive Rental & Leasing

Organizations primarily involved in renting or leasing cars, trucks, and trailers without drivers to customers. This includes daily car rental agencies at airports and other locations, long-term auto leasing companies, and businesses that rent moving trucks or utility trailers.

### Commercial/Industrial Rental & Leasing

Organizations primarily involved in renting or leasing heavy equipment and machinery to businesses for commercial operations. This includes construction equipment rental, manufacturing machinery leasing, office equipment leasing, and other capital equipment that businesses need but don't want to purchase.

### Franchising & Other IP Leasing

Organizations primarily involved in granting rights to use intellectual property like trademarks, patents, and business methods in exchange for fees or royalties. This includes franchisors licensing their business models, companies licensing patents for manufacturing, and firms that own and lease other intellectual property rights.

### Personal & Household Goods Rental

### Rental Centers

### Automotive Repair & Maintenance

### Commercial/Industrial Equipment Repair & Maintenance

### Electronic & Precision Equipment Repair & Maintenance

## Resource Extraction

Organizations in the Resource Extraction industry are primarily involved in pulling natural resources from the earth including oil, gas, coal, metals, and minerals. This includes drilling, mining, and the support services that help extract these materials.

### Coal Mining

Organizations primarily involved in extracting coal from underground or surface mines and preparing it for use as fuel. This includes companies operating strip mines, underground coal mines, and facilities that clean, screen, and process coal to meet market specifications.

### Crude Petroleum Extraction

Organizations primarily involved in drilling for and producing crude oil from underground reservoirs. This includes companies operating oil wells, extracting oil from shale or tar sands, and managing oil fields to bring petroleum to the surface for processing.

### Metal Ore Mining

Organizations primarily involved in extracting metallic minerals from the earth and processing them for further refining. This includes copper mines, gold mining operations, iron ore extraction, and facilities that crush, concentrate, and prepare metal ores for smelting.

### Mineral Mining

Organizations primarily involved in extracting non-metallic minerals except fuels from quarries and mines. This includes operations producing limestone, sand and gravel, clay, gypsum, salt, and other minerals used in construction and manufacturing.

### Natural Gas Extraction

Organizations primarily involved in drilling for and producing natural gas from underground formations. This includes companies operating gas wells, recovering liquid hydrocarbons from gas streams, extracting gas from shale formations, and capturing sulfur byproducts from natural gas.

### Support Activities for Mining

Organizations primarily involved in providing specialized services to assist mining operations on a contract basis. This includes drilling services, mine exploration, core sampling, mine site preparation, and other technical support that helps mining companies locate and extract resources.

## Transportation & Logistics

Organizations in the Transportation & Logistics industry are primarily involved in moving people and goods from place to place via air, water, road, rail, or pipeline. This also includes warehousing and storage services.

### Air Transportation

Organizations primarily involved in moving passengers or cargo by aircraft between locations. This includes commercial airlines, air cargo carriers, charter flight services, and helicopter transport companies that provide scheduled or on-demand air travel.

### Freight Trucking

Organizations primarily involved in hauling cargo over roads using trucks and tractor-trailers. This includes long-distance trucking companies, local delivery services, specialized carriers for specific types of freight, and owner-operators who transport goods by highway.

### Misc. Transportation Support, Maintenance, & Repair

Organizations primarily involved in supporting transportation operations without directly moving passengers or freight. This includes freight forwarders who arrange shipments, towing services, transportation equipment maintenance, and companies that manage logistics without owning vehicles.

### Pipeline Product Transportation

Organizations primarily involved in moving liquids and gases through pipeline systems. This includes companies operating oil pipelines, natural gas transmission lines, and other pipeline networks that transport petroleum products or other materials over long distances.

### Postal Service, Couriers, & Messengers

Organizations primarily involved in picking up and delivering letters, packages, and parcels. This includes national postal services, express delivery companies, local courier services, and messenger services that transport documents and small items.

### Rail Transportation

Organizations primarily involved in moving passengers or freight using trains on railroad networks. This includes freight railroads hauling cargo across long distances, passenger rail services between cities, and companies that operate railroad equipment and infrastructure.

### Scenic & Sightseeing Transportation

### Warehousing & Storage

### Electrical Utilities

### Steam & Air-Conditioning Supply

### Waste Management & Remediation Services

### Water & Sewage Utilities

Organizations primarily involved in treating and distributing drinking water or collecting and processing wastewater. This includes water treatment plants, water distribution systems, sewer networks, and sewage treatment facilities that serve communities.
